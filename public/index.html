GRIEF GARDEN

this digital memorial aims to hold space for climate grief.

there are two ways to engage with this exploratory virtual space:

• explore memorials: scroll through the site and click on cells with symbols to read more about memorials that others have shared, including essays, poems, and more.

• share a memorial: click on any non-water cell to add a memorial of your own. share anything about how you have experienced mourning and grief relating to climate -- a favorite tree, a beach, a friend, a feeling -- anything you would like to memorialize.

remember to step into this (virtual) space respectfully so that we may share our grief in community with one another.

GRIEF GARDEN is an exploratory space, containing stories of plants, animals, and other memorialized climate grief. Explore the canvas by clicking on cells to either learn more about a memorial or to add your own -- this can be a favorite tree, a beach, a friend, anything that you would like to memorialize.

Please be respectful of this space and of what others have shared as you enter so that we may share our grief in community.

GRIEF GARDEN was created by Kyle Barnes and Raya Ward and adapted from Permaculture Network by Gary Zhexi Zhang and Agnes Cameron. If you would like to request any additions or changes to the site and/or memorials, please contact us at ktbarnes@princeton.edu
